Step 3: Comprehensive Assessment

The analyses from Steps 1 and 2 are consistent in identifying a QSO at a redshift of approximately z ≈ 3.5. The initial Lyα identification is supported by the presence of a broad emission line at 5496.395 Å and the characteristic Lyα forest of narrow absorption lines blueward of this feature. Furthermore, other strong emission lines in the spectrum can be plausibly identified with common QSO UV features at a similar redshift, confirming the systemic redshift.

The redshift for each of the four primary emission lines was calculated individually:
- The line at 5496.395 Å, identified as Lyα (1215.67 Å), yields z = 3.521.
- The line at 7469.105 Å, identified as He II (1640.0 Å), yields z = 3.554.
- The line at 6777.185 Å, identified as C IV (1549.0 Å), yields z = 3.375.
- The line at 8433.378 Å, identified as C III] (1909.0 Å), yields z = 3.418.

The differences in redshift between the high-ionization lines (C IV, C III]) and the low-ionization lines (Lyα, He II) are a common feature in QSOs, caused by outflows that blueshift the high-ionization lines relative to the systemic velocity. To derive a systemic redshift, a weighted average of these four measurements was computed using their flux values (from the original, unsmoothed spectrum) as weights. This results in a final redshift of **z = 3.455 ± 0.072**.

Using this systemic redshift (z = 3.455), the predicted observed wavelengths for the confirmed emission lines are:
- **Lyα** (1215.67 Å): 5415.810 Å
- **He II** (1640.00 Å): 7306.200 Å
- **C IV** (1549.00 Å): 6900.795 Å
- **C III]** (1909.00 Å): 8504.595 Å

The observed wavelengths show offsets from these predictions, most notably for C IV and C III], which are blueshifted by ~124 Å and ~71 Å, respectively. This is consistent with the expected outflow signatures. The Lyα and He II lines are redshifted relative to the systemic value, which can occur due to radiative transfer effects in the broad-line region or a slight overestimation of the systemic redshift. Overall, the pattern of lines provides a robust confirmation of the QSO's redshift.
