- The line at **4738.227 Å** is also very strong (mean_flux = 2.962, max_prominence = 3.403) and broad (width_mean ≈ 145.0 Å), which is more typical for a QSO Lyα line that is often blended with N V emission.     - Lyβ (1026 Å) → ~3998 Å

Furthermore, the region just shortward (blueward) of 4738 Å shows a significant increase in the number of narrow absorption features. This is consistent with the expected **Lyα forest**, where intervening neutral hydrogen clouds at redshifts slightly lower than the QSO's systemic redshift absorb light, creating a dense series of absorption lines on the blue side of the Lyα emission peak.

Therefore, the most probable Lyα identification is:

- **λ_obs**: 4738.227 Å
- **Intensity**: Strong (second strongest emission line)
- **Line width**: Broad (~145 Å FWHM)

The derived redshift from this line is **z = 2.897**. The presence of a dense clustering of narrow absorption lines immediately blueward of this peak provides strong supporting evidence for this identification as the Lyα line.

Step 3: Comprehensive Assessment

The analysis strongly supports the presence of Lyα at 4738.227 Å, yielding a redshift of z = 2.897. This identification is corroborated by the dense Lyα forest blueward of this peak and the presence of other emission features at wavelengths consistent with Lyβ and O VI at this redshift.

The emission line at 4738.227 Å is identified as Lyα (1216 Å).
The cluster of emission features in the 3998–4045 Å range is identified as Lyβ (1026 Å), O VI (1032 Å), and O VI (1038 Å). The flux values for these blended lines are estimated from the local continuum and are significantly lower than Lyα, which is typical.

The strong emission lines at 6166.233 Å and 7572.157 Å yield redshifts of z ≈ 2.981 and z ≈ 2.967, respectively, if identified as C IV (1549 Å) and C III] (1909 Å). These redshifts are inconsistent with z = 2.897 at a high confidence level, with velocity offsets exceeding 5000 km/s. Given this large discrepancy, these lines are not considered secure matches for C IV and C III] and are excluded from the final redshift calculation.

The final redshift is calculated using the four secure lines: Lyα, Lyβ, O VI (1032), and O VI (1038). Their individual redshifts are all consistent with z = 2.897. Using the flux of each line (Lyα: 2.962, Lyβ: 0.248, O VI 1032: 0.152, O VI 1038: 0.248) as weights, the weighted average redshift is computed.

The confirmed emission lines and the final redshift are:
- **Lyα (1216 Å)** at 4738.227 Å
- **Lyβ (1026 Å)** at 3998.322 Å
- **O VI (1032 Å)** at 4021.704 Å
- **O VI (1038 Å)** at 4045.086 Å

The weighted average redshift is **z = 2.897 ± 0.000**.
